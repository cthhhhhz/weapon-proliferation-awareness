About Weapon Proliferation

Weapon proliferation refers to the spread of weapons across international borders, both through legal arms trade and illegal trafficking. This issue contributes to conflicts, violence, and instability around the world, affecting not only countries in war but also civilians and communities.

Global Impact

Globally, the proliferation of weapons increases violence, prolongs wars, and causes civilian casualties. The availability of weapons makes conflicts more destructive and peace more difficult to achieve.

Indonesia Perspective

Indonesia faces challenges in controlling illegal weapon movement due to its large number of islands and extensive maritime borders. Monitoring sea routes and preventing smuggling activities are important to maintain national security and stability.

Our Research Process

Our team conducted research using international reports, academic articles, and global news sources. We collaborated online, analyzed global and local perspectives, and combined our findings to understand the impact of weapon proliferation across international borders.

Solutions

Reducing weapon proliferation requires stronger international regulations, improved border security, and cooperation between countries. Raising awareness is also important to help people understand the global impact of weapon proliferation.
